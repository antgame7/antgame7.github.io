Quality Assurance

Quality Assurance represents all of the effort performed to ensure our work product works properly in the field. Our QA process ensures that users get the user experience they expect. It just works. We automate the testing process with some of the most advanced technologies available.
Unless the customers tells us not to, our work will always be covered with 100% branch and test coverage.

  • Human-based software testing

    Our engineers get into the shoes of the software's users. They think like they think, they act like they act. This helps us cover the hidden requirements that customers often have, but don't know how to express.

  • Automated testing

    We use the most advanced tools and techniques in the industry to verify the customer and user experience is excellent. For instance, we screenshot the pages and the elements on the page between every single change. This ensures they render and perform exactly as they should across many different devices and resolutions (mobile, tablet, desktop, laptop...). With our technologies and techniques, hundreds of pages can be analyzed and reviewed for changes or issues in seconds.

  • Performance testing

    Performance testing is all about how an application performs under extreme load. What happens when 10,000 users hit your site simultaneously on the release of a new sales offering? We make it work beautifully.

Business analysis

Business analysis represents the most important stage in the development of any product or service offering. Business analysis focuses on how we take needs of the business, stakeholders, customers, and users and translate them into objectives that people can understand. What most people don't understand about the Software Development Lifecycle (SDLC) is that it is a process of learning. If sales and engineering can't speak the same language, how can they develop a product that helps one another?

  • Where are we now and where do we want to be?

    Everything starts from where you are today. If a business wants an invoicing process that takes days to take minutes, we can expect that there is a lot of room for improvement in the process and the way information is transferred between individuals. If they have trouble tracking inventory, we can expect there are issues with accuracy and feedback loops. More often than not, it's the things we do that we don't know we do, that make our business work.

  • What does this cost?

    We show you how to do cost-benefit analysis like a CFO, so you can get exactly what you need for the budget you have in mind.

  • What does this look like?

    We give you stories and designs that you can click through to see how your process works today, and how it should work tomorrow. We make it easy for everyone to understand exactly what they're seeking.
